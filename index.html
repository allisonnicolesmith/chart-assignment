New York City's Latest Megadevelopment Touts Affordable Housing. Opponents Beg to Differ.

A luxury development project would rezone the waterfront in Flushing, Queens, but concerns over its affordability remain.

By Allison Nicole Smith | November 13, 2020

A proposed Special Flushing Waterfront District would transform 29 acres of the waterfront into a 13-tower luxury complex with retail, hotel and office space. Expressed benefits cited by the developers include 1,725 new apartments to the area, 61 units of which would be affordable, as required by zoning regulations. However, data raises questions about how affordable these units are.

The median household income within a quarter-mile of the SFWD is $28,988. Within the same area, 32 percent of households are at or below poverty level.
